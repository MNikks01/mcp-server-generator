# MCP Server Generator — SALES (templates for the first ₹10,000)

> Copy-paste outreach templates for a solo founder with no audience. The first ₹10,000 comes from **done-for-you gigs + Pro conversions**, sourced by **personal DMs and emails** to agencies, freelancers, and SaaS founders. Keep messages short, specific, and human. Pairs with [FIRST_CUSTOMERS.md](./FIRST_CUSTOMERS.md), [GTM.md](./GTM.md), [MONETIZATION.md](./MONETIZATION.md).

## 0. The rules of outreach
1. **Personalize the first line** (mention their actual project/API). No first line about them = delete.
2. **Lead with value, not the ask** (the free tool + a genuine offer to help).
3. **Short.** 3–5 sentences. A busy dev won't read a pitch.
4. **One clear CTA.** Either "try this" or "want me to build it?"
5. **Do things that don't scale** — 50 personal messages beat any automation.

## 6. Asking for the sale (to a happy free user)
> Glad it worked! Two things: (1) want me to set up your *real* production one (premium descriptions, all endpoints, pushed to GitHub)? (2) mind if I share a quick screenshot of you using it / a one-line testimonial? Helps a ton.

## 7. Asking for a testimonial / referral
> Thanks for [buying/trying] — would you mind a 1-line testimonial I can put on the site? And if you know anyone else wiring an API into Claude/agents, an intro would mean a lot. 🙏

---

## 8. The 30-day sales motion (numbers)
- **Build a list of 50** specific targets (handle, where found, their API) in a spreadsheet.
- **Send 10/day** during launch week (templates 1–3), personalized.
- **Expected funnel:** 50 sent → ~10–20 replies → ~3–6 interested → **1–2 done-for-you ($40–100)** + a few Pro/one-time.
- **₹10,000 (~$120):** 1–2 done-for-you gigs clear it; Pro + one-time top it up.
- **Track:** messages sent, replies, calls, paid — in the same spreadsheet. Paying customers + ₹, not vanity metrics.

## 9. What NOT to do
- No mass/automated DMs (spam = banned + reputation hit).
- No long pitches.
- No ads.
- Don't be shy about the paid + done-for-you ask — that's literally where the first ₹10,000 is.
