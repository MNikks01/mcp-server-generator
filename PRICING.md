## Logic
- **Generation is ~free** → maximize adoption + funnel into #2/#1. The OSS core makes local generation truly free.
- **Hosting is the revenue** → priced by usage (containers + traffic) above included credits; this is the COGS, so meter it.
- **Team** = collaboration + templates + governance + observability.
- **Enterprise** = registry + SSO + on-prem + governance.
- Annual discount ~2 months; spend caps + transparency on hosting.

## Why give generation away
The generated code is the customer's and runs anywhere (no lock-in) — so we don't gate generation; we earn on *hosting + collaboration + governance*, and on the strategic funnel value (leads for Codebase Intelligence + ContextOS). The free generator is marketing that happens to be a product.

## Expansion levers
More hosted servers + traffic, team seats, codebase input, marketplace, enterprise governance.

## Experiments
Free generation cap (funnel vs. abuse); hosting price points; per-seat vs. flat Team; whether to charge for codebase input separately.
